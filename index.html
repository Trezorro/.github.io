Proposal for utilizing Slack as main communications platform in DSA Pattern

Ever since we've started the founding process of DSA Pattern, we've tried lots and lots of ways to quickly and clearly communicate between commitees and groups and collaborate on our different projects in a well structured manner. Since the beginning, most committees have been almost completely reliant on whatsapp for planning meetings, discussing progress and sharing minutes and other files. Unfortunately, the more we rely on whatsapp for team collaboration, the more clearly it shows it's pittfalls and shortcomings. You see, whatsapp wasn't designed for professional team communication at all. Rather, it started as a simple and free alternative to texting. (you remember texting??) This has often made it very hard to relocate shared files, look up statements and agreements that were made in the past, let alone trying to achieve a clear overview of project progression. It's fairly annoying how often a question or idea shared in a whatsapp group just gets ignored and forgotten, but it's just impossible to assign and manage actual responsibilities through whatsapp, requiring the group to organise entire recurring meetings instead.

The second medium of communication which we have depended on is of course email. It's much better for sharing files, it's clearer for communicating and assigning responsibilities and it let's you search through conversations with much more precision. However, it lacks in ease of use and is limited by the more formal and slower way of messaging compared to whatsapp. Result: it's never used internally by committees. Moreover, nobody likes an inbox full of email that, if you want to avoid an insurmountable mess, needs to be sorted and prioritized over and over again...

Above all else, these 2 ways of communicating digitally make it extremely hard to achieve and maintain structure and transparency in the communication between all of the committees and members of Pattern. This limits how easily we can share ideas, collaborate, organise projects and events, and how quickly and efficiently we can reach all of our goals.

These issues got me thinking and searching for an alternative. I had heard about Slack before, but once I really started looking into it, that's when I got really excited. So then I begun writing up this little proposal, which I address to all active members of Pattern:


Let's start using Slack as central platform for communication and team collaboration within our association Pattern.

Here's a short promotional video made by Slack:

This is what Slack can help us with:

  • Communicating transparently across the association
  • Providing greater visibility into what other teams are working on
  • Quicker feedback and better decision making
  • Easy access to information, documents, and files
  • Bringing all our online tools and services into one place and shuffling and searching less between different windows and systems
  • Integrate powerful tools for the most tech savy students in our midst.

So... what exactly is Slack?

Slack is an established, innovative, flexible and intuitive communications platform that enables effective collaboration and communication for all kinds of business structures. It can be used for free via their universal apps and is widely used by professional teams and corporations from all over the world.

To get a very quick and comprehensive impression of how Slack works, please take a look at the following video. Actually, it's part of a playlist, you can keep watching and get to know a lot more, if you'd like to.

What are the advantages over normal IM or email apps?

Even though it really can be used for free, it might be hard to imagine how an app like Slack might actually be worth using next to all your other apps. Well, there's no way to really know until you try it out with a team and see how it comes together in practice. However, I can easily list a couple of cool features that might already sound useful to you:

Channels

Channels are the basic tool for structuring communications in Slack. Channels are generally open for anyone in the slack team to join, but private channels can also be used when necessary.

Channels can be about anything. The key is to keep conversations and shared items on-topic, so you only have to pay attention to channels that are relevant for you. It doesn't matter whether the subject is #funnypictures or an upcoming #project-lunchlecture. As long as you use the right channel, you can share anything without harming productivity.

Direct messaging

If you only need to adress someone or a group something that doesn't belong in any channel, you can just DM or group message them. These conversations are always private.

Share meeting notes and next steps in project channels

Share regular updates and meeting notes in a standard format so they’re easy to scan. Use checkboxes in posts to assign and track action items. Allow others to edit your post so they can record when their action items are complete.

Notify the right people to keep the noise to a minimum

Use @-mentions to notify team members and User Groups of important updates and ask them to confirm receipt with emoji reactions.

Share files and get feedback quickly all in one place

Share documents that need input or approval and get the right people involved. Comments are threaded so all feedback stays attached to the original document.

Pinning

Pin important messages and files (e.g. project briefs, designs, minutes, task lists) so it’s easy for anyone to join and quickly get up to speed.

Incredibly powerful and flexible search

One of the major benefits of Slack is how easy it makes it for you to find back anything you recieved or read in the past. Search queries are incredibly versitile thanks to search modifiers like before:[date] or in:[channel].

You can even set automatic notifications for when a certain word is mentioned anywhere.

Service and app integration

Connect Slack with file sharing services like Box, Dropbox, and Google Drive so your team always has the most recent version of any file. Your original sharing preferences are preserved so you can share without compromising security. Or integrate an app like Trello, to really get on top of your group's progress.

And you can easily plan events and be reminded of upcoming meetings by google calendar, right from within Slack.

Use powerful bots to help your group work together

To-do bot or meekan are great examples. There are bots for taking polls, planning meetings, analysing data, even bots for giving other team members credit for good work or a helping hand. Bots also have great potential to integrate with our own systems in the future, as they can be easily programmed by anyone.

Mobile apps

And of course, you can access Slack from wherever you are, just by downloading their apps on your phone or laptop. So you can keep on chatting, just like you were when using whatsapp, but cooler.

Conclusion

Using slack could improve transparency and productivity across committees and groups and make our association forever more responsive and dynamic. It will make it easier for the board to manage committees and improve productivity within them.

This is the potential of Slack. Still, for integrating slack into our association's workflow to be succesfull, we first need to agree that it is the best way to go, and if so, agree on some guidelines on how exactly we use the tools and tricks that slack provides for us. This includes how we name channels, what integrated services we use, and who gets permission to view which channels. This will be an iterative progress, but we can start experimenting right now.

In order to find out what the desired workflow and use cases of Slack would look like for Pattern, I would propose assigning one or two committees to take part as a pilot group, and have them use Slack consistently for all team communication. We can then easily evaluate the experience and productivity, identifying issues and places where additional guidelines are needed.

Today, Pattern is still small. If we decide adopting the Slack platform is the best way to go, we now have the perfect opportunity to do so. It could impact the way our association functions for years to come, and hopefully for the better.

And so, I ask you for your opinion. If there are no serious objections, we will start using Slack very soon with a few committees. So let me know if you are excited to try it out! You can reply by email, whatsapp, or on Slack itself, because our dsa Pattern team page has actually already been created!

Cheers,
